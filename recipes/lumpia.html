Lumpiang Shanghai

Description

One of the best Filipino foods next to pancit. These spring rolls are filled with a savory mix of ground pork, finely chopped vegetables and aromatic spices, all wrapped in a delicate, golden-brown wrapper. Served with a side of sweet and tangy dipping sauce, each bite delivers a perfect balance of crunch and taste, making it an irresistible appetizer or snack.

Ingredients

  • Lumpia Wrappers
  • Ground Pork
  • Finely chopped onions
  • Finely chopped carrots
  • Finely chopped scallions
  • Cooking Oil

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, blend together the ground pork, onion, scallions and carrot. Add spices to taste.
  2. Lay several lumpia wrappersout on a clean dry surface and cover with a damp towel.
  3. Place about 2 tablespoons of the meat mixture along the center of the wrapper. The filling should be no bigger around than your thumb or the wrapper will burn before the meat is cooked. Fold one edge of the wrapper over to the other. Fold the outer edges in slightly, then continue to roll into a cylinder. Wet your finger, and moisten the edge to seal.
  4. Heat oil in a skillet at medium to medium high heat. Fry 3 to 4 lumpia at a time. It should take about 2 to 3 minutes for each side. The lumpia will be nicely browned when done.
  5. Drain excess oil using paper towels.
